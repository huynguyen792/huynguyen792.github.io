About

I am a Research Fellow in the School of Electrical Engineering & Robotics at the Queensland University of Technology, within the SAIVT research program. I completed my PhD at QUT in 2026, advised by Dr Kien Nguyen Thanh, Prof. Clinton Fookes, and Emeritus Prof. Sridha Sridharan.

My research is on person re-identification in the wild — matching the same person across cameras that see them from completely different vantage points and sensors. I develop deep-learning architectures and large-scale benchmarks that bridge aerial UAV and ground perspectives, span image, video, and cross-modality (visible–infrared) settings, and aim to be interpretable rather than black-box. My PhD thesis, Bridging Aerial and Ground Perspectives, received an outstanding examiners' recommendation and an ODTA nomination.

Datasets & Benchmarks

AG-VPReID.VIR

First aerial-ground visible–infrared video Re-ID dataset — cross-modality matching across UAV and ground platforms. (IJCB 2025, Best Paper)

AG-VPReID

Large-scale aerial-ground video Re-ID benchmark: 6,632 subjects, 32,321 tracklets, 9.6M+ frames from drones (15–120 m), CCTV, and wearable cameras. (CVPR 2025)

AG-ReID.v2

Image-based aerial-ground Re-ID benchmark bridging UAV, CCTV, and wearable views. (TIFS 2024)

AG-ReID

The original image-based aerial-ground Re-ID benchmark (~100K images, 1,600+ identities). (ICME 2023)
